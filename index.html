INTRODUCTION

Remembering & Reflecting

It's been about two months since I first arrived at Princeton: two months since I stood at the edge of the window in my room, crying silently to Taylor Swift's iconic "22" being blasted from First Courtyard, all while trying to get a last glimpse of my family as they drove away.


In what proved to be the busiest, scariest, and happiest two months of my life, I gradually began to let go of some of the caution, worry, and doubt that I unconsciously brought with me. I may have changed a bit along the way, but those changes were small and felt alright; I tried to spend more time outside; I tried to smile more often; I gave more hugs. Princeton, once a distant dream, had somehow become my reality, and I began to feel less uneasy about that.


As Fall Break approached, I gave very little thought to the prospect of going home. Midterms played a part, but the other part was just a sense of prolonged distance from the life I'd had when I'd been at home. Princeton didn't quite feel like "home" to me (yet?), but at the same time, I felt like a vastly different person from the girl who called that red-brick house "home". Midterms went by surprisingly quickly, though, and sooner than I realized, I was back in the very car that I'd watched drive away two months ago.


Being home has been interesting. Knowing that I'll be leaving in a week, I try not to get too used to it, but I also try to take in and appreciate every moment while I'm here. Sometimes, a certain angle of the light or a specific smell tosses back memories I didn't realize I'd left behind, and for a brief moment I'll let them creep into the present.


Sometimes, I'll even catch them in a photo.
